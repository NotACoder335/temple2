About The Temple

History & Legends

Believed to be over a thousand years old, the Pulikkathara Sree Vishnu Maya-Bhadrakali Temple is intertwined with the rich mythological tapestry of Kerala. Legends say the original idol (prathishta) was consecrated by sage Parasurama.

Over centuries, it has stood as a beacon of spiritual solace, surviving natural calamities and preserving the ancient tantric traditions.

Main Deity (Prathishta)

The presiding deity is Lord Shiva, worshipped in the form of a Shiva Lingam. The sanctum sanctorum (Sreekovil) is circular, a unique architectural marvel reflecting traditional Kerala vasthu shastra.

Sub-Deities (Upadevatas)

  • Lord Ganesha (Ganapathi)
  • Lord Ayyappa (Sastha)
  • Goddess Parvathi (Bhagavathi)
  • Naga Devatas (Serpent Gods)
